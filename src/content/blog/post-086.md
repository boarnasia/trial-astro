---
title: 'Managing Technical Debt'
description: 'Learn how to identify, prioritize, and systematically pay down technical debt without halting feature work.'
pubDate: 'Sep 03 2022'
---

Technical debt is the accumulated cost of shortcuts taken to deliver features faster. Some technical debt is acceptable — the business benefit of shipping quickly sometimes outweighs the maintenance cost. The problem is when debt goes unacknowledged and compounds.

The first step is making debt visible. An explicit list of known debt items, prioritized by impact, allows the team to make informed trade-offs. Hidden debt accumulates because it is never discussed or weighed against other priorities.

Paying down debt incrementally — the Boy Scout Rule of leaving code better than you found it — prevents the need for large-scale rewrites. Allocating a fixed percentage of each sprint to debt reduction keeps it from being permanently deferred without disrupting feature delivery.
