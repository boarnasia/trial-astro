---
title: 'Understanding WCAG 2.1 Guidelines'
description: 'A developer-friendly breakdown of WCAG 2.1 success criteria and how to meet them in your projects.'
pubDate: 'Mar 11 2023'
---

WCAG 2.1 defines success criteria for web accessibility at three conformance levels: A, AA, and AAA. Most organizations target AA compliance, which is also what most legal requirements reference.

Level A covers the most basic requirements — text alternatives for images, captions for video, keyboard accessibility, and no content that flashes more than three times per second. These are things that completely block access for users with disabilities.

Level AA adds requirements for minimum contrast ratios, resizable text, visible focus indicators, consistent navigation, and error identification in forms. Meeting AA compliance requires deliberate effort but is achievable with good development practices.
