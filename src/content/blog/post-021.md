---
title: 'Building a Design System from Scratch'
description: 'A practical walkthrough of creating a scalable design system including tokens, components, and documentation.'
pubDate: 'Sep 13 2021'
---

A design system is more than a component library — it is the shared language between design and engineering. It includes visual tokens, component implementations, usage guidelines, and the processes for keeping everything in sync.

Start by auditing your existing UI to find patterns. What colors appear repeatedly? What spacing values are most common? What components are rebuilt in multiple places? This audit reveals the building blocks of your design system.

Documentation is as important as the components themselves. Each component should document its intended use cases, available props, accessibility considerations, and examples of correct and incorrect usage. Without good documentation, even a well-built design system will not be adopted.
